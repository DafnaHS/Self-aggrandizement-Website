Self-aggrandizement

 

What is self-aggrandizement?

Self-aggrandizement is the act of making oneself more powerful, wealthy, and other things, in a ruthless way. Self-aggrandizement is a colloquial term that broadly describes Narcissism, the most defining trait of people with Narcissistic Personality Disorder. It differs from general boasting, which most of us indulge in now and then, in that it can often manifest in aggressive, ruthless, or manipulative patterns of relating. The opposite of self-aggrandizement is self-deprecating which is is the act of reprimanding oneself by belittling, undervaluing, or disparaging oneself, or being excessively modest. 

Some psychologists believe that Narcissists are people whose emotional centers have fewer connections to the higher reasoning parts of the brain located in the frontal cortex - so they lack that filter most of us use to keep our basic instincts in check. Despite their facade, self-aggrandizers often suffer from low self-esteem themselves. They live in a dog-eat-dog world of fear where being anything less than being number one is regarded as a failure – a world where nobody can be trusted.

What it Looks Like

  • A person engages in false accusations, smear campaigns, distortion campaigns, character assassination attempts, and malicious gossip.
  • An employee habitually gravitates to or assumes positions of power, authority, or leadership while disregarding the existence of more qualified individuals.
  • A friend amplifies their own image or behaves in a pompous, arrogant manner.
  • A team member tenaciously avoids playing anonymous roles or engaging in thankless tasks.
  • A family member frequently speaks out inappropriately, drawing attention to themselves.
  • A parent inappropriately puts their own needs or interests above the needs of their children.
